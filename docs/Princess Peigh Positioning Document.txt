MODULE 1 — Core Identity
Show Identity

Princess Peigh’s Sword Fighting Tea Party is a live, interactive family stage show built for ages 4 to 8, designed to entertain children while resonating deeply with adults.

It is not a character meet-and-greet.
It is not a birthday party act.
It is not a parody princess show.

It is a tightly scripted theatrical experience disguised as a whimsical tea party.

At its heart, the show is about:

Self-worth

Emotional intelligence

Breaking expectations

Courage

Curiosity

Good sportsmanship

Being unapologetically yourself

The show ends with a communal affirmation:
“I Am Enough.”

That is the brand anchor.

Brand Positioning

Princess Peigh is positioned as:

An original theatrical property that subverts traditional princess tropes while remaining accessible, joyful, and marketable.

Core differentiators:

Original IP, not character impersonation

Princess who is eccentric, curious, imperfect, and self-aware

Integrated stage combat finale

Structured interactive theatrical storytelling

Sensory-considerate and neurodivergent-aware design

Plays equally well in theaters, libraries, and PACs

This is a small-cast touring theatrical production with festival potential.

Founders’ Dynamic

MODULE 2 — Performance Philosophy
Theatrical Philosophy

The show operates under one core belief:

Children deserve emotionally intelligent theater that treats them as capable humans.

Princess Peigh does not preach.
She models.

Lessons are embedded inside theatrical traditions such as:

A tea set that does not work

A canceled entertainer forcing adaptation

A human instrument activity

Hidden talents revealed

An epic sword fight that models sportsmanship

The message is revealed through action, not lecture.

Audience Psychology

The primary age range is 4 to 8.

Design principles:

Clear narrative structure

Visual physical comedy

Musical cues instead of loud sudden effects

Repetition and callbacks

Participation moments with controlled structure

Adults are included intentionally through layered humor and thematic depth.

Children experience the fun.
Adults recognize the emotional architecture.

Neurodivergent Integration

The show is built with lived experience awareness.

Features:

Predictable rhythm

No sudden loud noises

Music-driven transitions

Clear emotional framing

Sensory-friendly adaptations available

Performers trained to respond in-character to unexpected contributions

This is not labeled as a “special needs show.”
It is inclusive by default.
